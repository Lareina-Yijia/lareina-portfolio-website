I accidentally saw a post, and someone asked, "Does anyone know the name of the safe evacuation of the little green person?" Another person replied, "Mr. Picto." This post attracted me and prompted me to look up the information. This can be traced back to the 1970s, when several large fires broke out in Japanese buildings, and the fire department believed that there was a lack of a prominent sign that could evacuate people in emergency situations, transforming it into a sign for mass mobilization. So Mr. Picto was born.

Let's Draw is a community painting activity project in Nangong. When conducting research in the Nangong community, we found that firstly, the barriers between each community in the Nangong community are very thick, and they do not frequently interact and communicate with each other. Secondly, on weekends, there will be many children not only playing in their respective neighborhoods, but also playing and playing together in the Y-shaped alleys in the community. These two points make us ponder whether the barriers between communities can also be broken by children.

So we have Let's Draw! Using paintbrushes to introduce the Nangong project, we hope to use children's paintbrushes to help those who have lived in the community for a long time but have not seen it well, calm down and take a serious look at the beautiful little things in the community. Using children's paintings as a topic for communication among residents.

Let's Draw is divided into two activities. Activity 1: release tweets, call the children in Nangong Community to draw the objects in the community, and send their pictures to Let us Draw on the official account. At the same time, carry out offline activities and invite children to paint on-site in the community. Activity 2: Arrange the children's paintings into a detachable collection of works, and upload the collection to the official account. At the same time, paste the children's paintings in the community. This event and the booklet produced have created an opportunity for residents of Nangong to exchange ideas, increase topic awareness, and serve as a brochure for Nangong to help residents better understand Nangong.

Let's Draw this time! Introducing the Nangong activity with a paintbrush is the beginning of our community project, and we also hope to use Let's Draw in the future! Initiate more community activities as a slogan.
